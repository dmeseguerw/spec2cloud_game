# 📋 Feature Design Document: Quest & Objectives System

## 1. Feature Overview

### Feature Name
**Quest & Objectives System**

### Purpose
The Quest & Objectives System is the answer to the player's most fundamental question: *"What do I do now?"* Without it, a player who finishes talking to Lars has no visible path forward. With it, the game always has a gentle finger pointing toward the next meaningful action — not heavy-handed enough to feel like being hand-held, but present enough that the player is never lost.

The system serves two purposes simultaneously: it **guides new players** through their first hours by surfacing story-driven goals, and it **structures experienced players' sessions** by generating meaningful daily maintenance tasks based on their actual game state. It does both without pop-up tutorials or intrusive UI — the Objectives Panel is always visible but never in the way.

This is the connective tissue that makes every other system in the game feel like part of a coherent experience rather than a collection of isolated features.

### Priority
- [x] **Critical** (MVP — must exist before the game leaves tutorial)

### Dependencies
- Day/Night Cycle (generates Daily Maintenance tasks each new day)
- Dialogue & NPC System (NPC resolution of dialogue nodes can push Story Missions)
- XP & Progression System (completing objectives awards XP; level ups may unlock new mission types)
- State Management (all active tasks, completed tasks, and mission progress must persist across sessions)
- Inventory & Economy System (objectives may require specific items or financial states)

## 2. Player-Facing Design

### Player Actions
1. **Glance at the Objectives Panel** — the active task is always visible at the bottom-centre of the HUD
2. **Expand to full task list** — click/tap the panel to expand and see all active tasks (Story Missions and Daily tasks)
3. **Track a specific task** — pin a task as "active" so it displays prominently in the HUD and is pointed toward on the (future) map
4. **Mark a task as "skip for today"** — on optional Daily tasks only; removes it from the panel for today's session
5. **Review completed tasks** — via the Pause Menu (stats/journal), see all tasks completed to date

---

### Visual Design

#### The Objectives Panel (HUD — Always Visible)
- Located **bottom-centre** of the screen, horizontally centred
- At rest, shows a single line: the currently tracked/active task
- Format: `[Icon] Task description` where the icon indicates task type (📖 Story Mission, 🔵 Daily Task, ⚠️ Urgent)
- The panel uses a minimal semi-transparent dark background with rounded corners — visible without blocking gameplay
- Text is clean and brief (max ~6 words): "Buy groceries from Netto" not "You need to purchase food items from the Netto supermarket on your street"
- When a task is completed: the text briefly turns green, a small checkmark appears, and the text is replaced by the next active task within 2 seconds
- When the player has no active tasks: the panel shows a soft prompt: *"No tasks right now — explore!"*

#### Expanded Task List (Click to Open)
- Slides up from the Objectives Panel into a compact floating panel — does not cover the full screen
- Two sections, clearly separated:

  **Story Missions** (gold header, 📖 icon)
  - Listed in priority order (current chapter's main mission at top)
  - Each entry shows: mission name, brief description, current progress if applicable (e.g., "Talk to Lars — 1/1 ✅")
  - Completed missions shown in faded gold with a checkmark — visible history

  **Daily Tasks** (blue header, 🔵 icon)
  - Maintenance tasks generated by current day state
  - Optional tasks shown with a small *(optional)* label
  - Each entry shows: task name, brief description, due indicator if urgent (e.g., "Rent due in 1 day ⚠️")

- Clicking any task "pins" it as the active tracked task
- Panel can be dismissed by clicking elsewhere, pressing Tab again, or pressing Escape

#### Task Completion Feedback
- When a task completes: a brief **golden flash** on the Objectives Panel + satisfying **chime sound**
- The checkmark animation lasts ~1.5 seconds before the next task slides in
- If the completed task was a **Story Mission**, a slightly more dramatic effect: the panel expands briefly with the mission name and a "Mission Complete!" label, then collapses with the next task

#### Urgent Task Warning
- If the player has an urgent task (e.g., bill overdue, food supply at zero) and the Objectives Panel is collapsed:
  - The panel gently **pulses amber** every 30 seconds
  - The ⚠️ icon replaces the 📖 or 🔵 icon on the active task line
  - This is noticeable but not alarming — a nudge, not a siren

---

### Audio Design
- **Task assigned** (new mission added): Soft, warm 2-note chime. Gold tone. Sounds like "something important just happened."
- **Daily task generated**: Subtle single chime. Quieter. Sounds like "here's what you need to do today."
- **Task completed** (regular): Short 3-note ascending chime. Satisfying without being over-the-top.
- **Story Mission completed**: 4-note fanfare. Warm and slightly triumphant. Not as big as a level-up, but noticeably more significant than a regular task.
- **Urgent task warning**: A quiet single low-tone pulse every 30 seconds. Clock-like but not anxious.

## 3. Rules & Mechanics

### Task Types

#### Story Missions
Story Missions advance the game's Chapter narrative. They are **always authored** — crafted for specific moments in the story, not generated algorithmically.

**Properties:**
- One active Story Mission at a time (in Chapter 1; later chapters may chain 2-3 simultaneously)
- Given to the player via NPC dialogue — the dialogue node that triggers the mission is explicitly tagged with the mission ID
- Never expire — they remain active until completed or the story moves past them
- Completion almost always involves: talking to a specific NPC, entering a specific location, or completing a specific action
- Always award XP on completion (15–50 XP depending on significance)

**Chapter 1 Story Mission Chain (Days 1–14):**

| Day | Mission Name | Trigger | Goal | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *Grocery Run* | Lars's opening dialogue | Buy groceries from Netto | +15 XP, opens Netto |
| 2 | *First Class* | Lars's Day 1 closing line | Attend language school intro | +25 XP, +1 Language Skill XP |
| 3–4 | *Meet the Commute* | Sofie mentions the metro | Take the metro for the first time | +20 XP, Metro access opened |
| 5–6 | *Clock In* | Morning reminder | Attend your first work day | +30 XP, Salary introduced |
| 7 | *One Week In* | Day 7 auto-trigger | Survive your first week | +50 XP, Day Summary milestone |
| 8–10 | *The Neighbour* | Thomas walks past | Have a second conversation with Thomas | +25 XP, Thomas relationship +10 |
| 11–13 | *The Pant Run* | Mette mentions the return system | Return 5 pant bottles | +20 XP, pant system tutorial complete |
| 14 | *Settled?* | Lars invites the player for kaffe | Accept Lars's coffee invitation | +40 XP, Lars relationship 60/100 |

*Each mission in this chain is designed to introduce a new system or location while feeling narratively organic.*

#### Daily Maintenance Tasks
Daily tasks are **generated each new day** based on the player's current state. They represent the ongoing responsibilities of daily life. Unlike Story Missions, they feel less authored and more emergent — "this is your life right now."

**Generation Rules:**
- Generated at the start of each in-game day
- Each task checks a specific game state condition
- Each active condition generates one task — multiple can be active simultaneously (max 4 daily tasks at once)
- Tasks marked "skip for today" by the player are not regenerated until the underlying condition changes

**Daily Task Triggers:**

| Condition | Task Generated | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| Food supply ≤ 1 item | *"You're running low on food — visit a shop"* | ⚠️ Urgent |
| Food supply = 0 | *"You have nothing to eat — buy groceries now"* + XP loss if ignored | 🚨 Critical |
| Bill due in 2 days | *"[Bill name] is due in 2 days — pay it soon"* | Normal |
| Bill overdue | *"[Bill name] is overdue — pay it now to avoid penalty"* | ⚠️ Urgent |
| No vitamin D in inventory | *"You're out of vitamin D — pick some up at Matas"* | Normal (Winter: ⚠️ Urgent) |
| Player health < 40% | *"You're not feeling well — consider resting or eating"* | Normal |
| Player hasn't used transport in 3+ days | *"It's a nice day — maybe try biking to [active location]?"* | Low/Optional |
| An NPC has new dialogue (story-relevant) | *"[NPC name] seems to want to talk to you"* | Normal |
| A seasonal event is active | *"[Event name] is happening in [location] today!"* | Low/Optional |
| 5+ days since player last explored a new area | *"You haven't explored much lately — venture somewhere new?"* | Low/Optional |

**Optional vs Required:**
- Tasks marked 🚨 Critical or ⚠️ Urgent: ignoring these causes XP loss at end-of-day
- Tasks marked Normal: completing them earns XP, ignoring them has no penalty
- Tasks marked Low/Optional: bonus XP opportunity, no negative consequence for skipping

---

### Task Priority and Display Order

When multiple tasks are active simultaneously, the Objectives Panel cycles through them on a **5-second rotation** (showing one at a time in the compact view). Display priority:

1. 🚨 Critical tasks
2. ⚠️ Urgent tasks
3. 📖 Active Story Mission
4. 🔵 Normal Daily tasks (highest urgency first)
5. Optional Daily tasks

The player can override this by manually pinning any task.

---

### Mission Delivery — How NPCs Give Tasks

The mission-delivery mechanism is woven into the dialogue system. Dialogue nodes that should assign a mission are tagged with a `missionAssign` effect.

**How it feels to the player:**
An NPC says something in natural dialogue. The conversation ends. The Objectives Panel updates with a gentle animation — the paper-handing-over gesture seen on Day 1 (Lars's grocery list) is the visual metaphor the player has already been taught. *Someone told you something to do, and now you know what to do.*

**Key Design Rule:** A mission must feel like it was *given* by someone, not *generated* by the game. Even algorithmically generated Daily tasks are framed as coming from the player character's own awareness ("you're nearly out of food") rather than from a game system. This maintains the fiction.

**Secondary Delivery — Environment:**
Some tasks can also be triggered by environmental interaction:
- Walking past a community notice board can surface an optional activity task
- Seeing a seasonal decoration in the city can surface a cultural event task
- Picking up a dropped item may create a task ("return this wallet — maybe someone dropped it nearby")

---

### Session End Behaviour

When the player ends a day (goes to sleep), any active Tasks that were:
- **Completed during the day:** Shown on the Day Summary with their XP rewards
- **Missed and Critical/Urgent:** Shown on the Day Summary with their XP penalties — clearly labelled *"not completed"*
- **Missed and Optional:** Not mentioned in the Day Summary (no guilt for skipping optional content)

The Day Summary also shows **what tasks are being created for tomorrow**, giving the player a concrete preview of the next session.

## 4. Edge Cases & Balance

**What if the player ignores all tasks?**
The world continues. Critical tasks that are ignored (no food, unpaid bills) accumulate XP losses and eventually trigger story beats that acknowledge the player's struggling situation (some NPCs may comment; the game may enter "assist mode"). The game does not punish passive play harshly, but deprivation has consequences over time.

**What if the player completes all tasks very early in the day?**
The Objectives Panel shows "No tasks right now — explore!" This is an intentional reward state. Free exploration time is a positive outcome for effective play. During these moments, random encounters are more likely — the game fills "idle time" with organic world events rather than manufactured busy-work.

**What if a Story Mission requires a location that is not yet accessible?**
This should never happen by design — Story Missions unlock locations before requiring the player to visit them. As a safeguard, if a Story Mission points to a locked location, the game first triggers the location-unlock condition, then assigns the mission.

**Can tasks conflict with each other?**
By design, no. The system does not simultaneously generate tasks that require the player to be in two places at once, or tasks that contradict each other (e.g., "save money" and "buy an expensive item" would never both appear as urgent tasks on the same day).

## 5. Acceptance Criteria

### Player Experience Goals
- At every point in the game, the player should be able to name at least one thing they could do next
- Objectives should feel like they come from the *world* rather than from a game system
- Completing a task should feel satisfying — the feedback should match the scale of the achievement
- Players should never need to "check a guide" to know what to do

### Functional Requirements
- The Objectives Panel must be visible at all times during gameplay (not hidden by default)
- At least one task must always be active (no state where the panel shows nothing indefinitely)
- Tasks must persist across game sessions (save/load must preserve state)
- Daily tasks must be generated at the start of each new in-game day
- Story Mission assignment must work through the dialogue `missionAssign` effect tag
- Completed tasks must be visible in the Pause Menu journal

### Success Metrics
- Less than 10% of players report "not knowing what to do" in post-play surveys
- 80%+ of players complete their active Story Mission on the same day it is assigned
- Task completion rate for Critical/Urgent tasks: 70%+ (indicating players are engaging with maintenance loops)
- Optional task completion rate: 40–60% (indicating optional content is attractive but genuinely optional)

## 6. Examples & References

**The Witcher 3 — Quest Log:** Sets the gold standard for multi-quest tracking — main quest, secondary quests, witcher contracts all clearly separated. The lesson: categorisation reduces cognitive load.

**Animal Crossing — Daily Objectives:** Chores and activities are suggested but never forced. The game feels richer for having things to do without demanding them. The lesson: optional tasks must feel rewarding, not ignored.

**Spiritfarer — Mission Chains:** Each character gives you tasks through conversation, not through UI pop-ups. The lesson: missions feel meaningful when they come from characters you care about.

**Stardew Valley — "Quests" Board:** Community centre bundles give long-term structure while daily life gives immediate tasks. The lesson: layered task types (long-term vs. same-day) create both momentum and satisfaction.
